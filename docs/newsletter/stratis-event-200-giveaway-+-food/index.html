In the News

Here are four noteworthy crypto related news articles:

Coinbase announces institutional staking custody

This past Friday, Coinbase announced that they’ll be offering platform for managing staked assets. The first asset they’ll support will be Tezos. No other coins have been mentioned up to today.

Justin Sun’s fake Tesla giveaway uncovered

Justin Sun, Tron’s founder recently announced a $20 million giveaway on twitter celebrating the launch of TRON’s new USDT token. He also featured a Tesla as one fo the prizes. The winner came out and said that he has not received anything and since then, Sun has deleted his tweet announcing him as the winner.

Japan’s largest railway company soon to accept crypto

East Japan Railway Company is japan’s largest railway company. They announced that they will be accepting cryptocurrency as a form of payment in the near future. Transactions will be facilitated by the DeCurret virtual currency exchange.

Crypto Exchange Bithumb Hacked for $13 Million

Spotted on Friday, Bithumb had around $13 million EOS stolen from them. They noticed an unusual withdrawal on Friday and suspect it may have been an inside job. Since then, they have suspended asset withdrawal and deposits on the platform.
